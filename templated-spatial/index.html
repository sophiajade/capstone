Relational Values and Land Ownership in King County, Washington

Sophia J. Brooks
University of Washington Tacoma MSGT Capstone

Abstract

Our appreciation of nature has changed in the context of what is most important to value, and how those values are fed into policymaking and conservation efforts. Concluding whether nature should be conserved and provide services for human benefit, or if it should remain in its wild state for the benefit of nature itself has been continually debated in policy discussions. The gap in these two notions suggests a hybrid method of showing the relational and qualitative values of certain spaces in nature, not often brought up in many decision-making discussions. Peri-urban agricultural lands in the Western United States hold significant value, due to rapid urbanization and growing development pressures. To approach a concept that is not circulated around policymaking as much as it should be, I will create multiple, comparable geospatial visualizations of the diverse forms of value placed on land. In doing so, the gaps in value will be the primary focus of this research project to show what is beyond just the economic benefits to peri-urban agriculture.
